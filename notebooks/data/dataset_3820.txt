This dataset contains QSAR data showing activity values of several compounds on drug target Progesterone Receptor, and it has multiple rows and features. The features represent molecular fingerprints which were generated from SMILES strings using the Pipeline Pilot program.

The dataset is part of the MetaQSAR project and was created in September 2015. It contains QSAR data from ChEMBL version seventeen, showing activity values of several compounds on drug target Progesterone Receptor. 

The preferred name of this target is Progesterone Receptor, also known as Nuclear Receptor Subfamily Three Group C Member Three, and it belongs to the species Homo sapiens. 

This target is classified as a transcription factor, specifically a nuclear receptor, and it plays a crucial role in various biological processes. 

There are multiple compounds that interact with this target, including Telapristone Acetate, Levonorgestrel, Ulipristal Acetate, Drospirenone, and Gestodene, among others. These compounds have different mechanisms of action and have been approved for various indications.

Expression Atlas provides information about the expression of this target in different tissues and cells. Component ontology reveals its cellular components, including chromatin, nucleus, nucleoplasm, cytoplasm, mitochondrion, and plasma membrane. 

Functional annotations indicate its involvement in various biological processes, including transcription, signal transduction, and cell-cell signaling. It also participates in the regulation of gene expression, specifically the activation and repression of transcription.
