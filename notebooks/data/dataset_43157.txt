Insurance Dataset

Tags: Machine Learning, Mathematics

Dataset Description:
The Insurance dataset is a comprehensive collection of data designed to evaluate car insurance risks.

Format of the Dataset:
The Insurance dataset comprises twenty-seven variables that provide a detailed understanding of various factors influencing car insurance risks.

Good Student is a binary factor indicating whether the policyholder is a good student or not. Age is a categorical variable with three levels: Adolescent, Adult, and Senior, representing different stages of life. Socio-Economic Status is a four-level factor categorizing individuals into Proletariat, Middle Class, Upper-Middle Class, and Wealthy groups. Risk Aversion is another four-level factor assessing an individual's attitude towards risk, ranging from Psychopathic to Cautious.

Vehicle Year indicates whether the vehicle is current or older. Damage to This Car is a four-level factor describing the extent of damage, ranging from None to Severe. Ruggedness of the Car categorizes vehicles into three levels: Eggshell, Football, and Tank, based on their durability. Accident Severity is a four-level factor assessing the severity of accidents, ranging from None to Severe.

Make and Model categorizes vehicles into five types: Sports Car, Economy, Family Sedan, Luxury, and Super Luxury. Driving Quality is a three-level factor evaluating driving skills, ranging from Poor to Excellent. Mileage is a four-level factor describing the distance traveled, categorized into Five Thousand, Twenty Thousand, Fifty Thousand, and Domino ranges. Antilock Braking System is a binary variable indicating whether the vehicle is equipped with ABS or not.

Driving Skill assesses the policyholder's driving abilities, categorized into Substandard, Normal, and Expert levels. Senior Training is a binary variable indicating whether the policyholder has undergone senior training or not. Costs for the Insured Car categorize expenses into four ranges: Thousand, Ten Thousand, Hundred Thousand, and Million. Theft is a binary variable indicating whether the vehicle was stolen or not. Car Value categorizes vehicle values into five ranges: Five Thousand, Ten Thousand, Twenty Thousand, Fifty Thousand, and Million.

Home Base describes the neighborhood type, categorized into Secure, City, Suburb, and Rural areas. Anti-Theft System is a binary variable indicating whether the vehicle is equipped with an anti-theft system or not. Property Cost Ratio describes the ratio of costs for the two cars involved in the accident, categorized into four ranges: Thousand, Ten Thousand, Hundred Thousand, and Million. Other Car Costs categorize expenses into four ranges: Thousand, Ten Thousand, Hundred Thousand, and Million. Other Car is a binary variable indicating whether another car was involved in the accident or not.

Medical Treatment Costs categorize expenses into four ranges: Thousand, Ten Thousand, Hundred Thousand, and Million. Cushioning describes the level of cushioning in the vehicle, categorized into four levels: Poor, Fair, Good, and Excellent. Airbag is a binary variable indicating whether the vehicle is equipped with an airbag or not. Inspection Costs categorize expenses into four ranges: Thousand, Ten Thousand, Hundred Thousand, and Million. Driving History assesses the policyholder's driving record, categorized into three levels: Zero, One, and Many accidents.

Keywords: Car Insurance, Risk Assessment, Vehicle Characteristics, Driver Profile, Accident Severity, Medical Treatment Costs.
