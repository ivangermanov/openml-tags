Disaster Tweets Tags: Context

The file contains a large collection of tweets associated with disaster keywords such as crash, quarantine, and bushfires, as well as the location and keyword itself. The data structure was inherited from Disasters on Social Media. The tweets were collected on a specific date. Some of the topics people were tweeting about include the eruption of Taal Volcano in Batangas, Philippines, the Coronavirus outbreak, and the devastating bushfires in Australia, as well as the Iran downing of the airplane flight.

Disclaimer: The dataset contains text that may be considered profane, vulgar, or offensive.

The intention behind creating this dataset was to enrich the already available data for this topic with newly collected and manually classified tweets. The initial source, Disasters on Social Media, was used in the Real or Not? NLP with Disaster Tweets competition on Kaggle.

The dataset features include the tweet identification, the keyword associated with the tweet, the location mentioned in the tweet, the text of the tweet itself, and the target of the tweet.

Keywords: disaster tweets, social media, natural disasters, crisis management, emergency response
