This dataset contains information on various environmental and demographic factors related to air pollution and mortality rates in urban areas. The data was collected from Standard Metropolitan Statistical Areas (SMSAs) in the United States.

Variables included in the dataset:

Average annual precipitation in inches
Average January temperature in degrees Fahrenheit
Average July temperature in degrees Fahrenheit
Percentage of SMSA population aged sixty-five or older
Average household size
Median school years completed by those over twenty-two
Percentage of housing units which are sound and with all facilities
Population per square mile in urbanized areas
Percentage of non-white population in urbanized areas
Percentage employed in white collar occupations
Percentage of families with low income
Relative hydrocarbon pollution potential
Relative nitric oxides pollution potential
Relative sulphur dioxide pollution potential
Annual average percentage relative humidity at one pm
Total age-adjusted mortality rate per one hundred thousand

The dataset is classified as numeric, with no specific class index. It is commonly used for studying the relationships between environmental factors, socioeconomic indicators, and mortality rates in urban areas.
