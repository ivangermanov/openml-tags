Internet Usage Data
Tags: UCI

This dataset contains general demographic information on internet users from 1997. The data comes from a survey conducted by the Graphics and Visualization Unit at Georgia Tech from October 10 to November 16, 1997. The full details of the survey are available online. The particular subset provided here focuses on the "general demographics" of internet users.

The data have been recoded as entirely numeric, with an index to the codes described in the "Coding" file. The full survey is available from the original website, along with summaries, tables, and graphs of their analyses. Additional information on other parts of the survey, including technology demographics and web commerce, is also available.

Data Characteristics:
The data is stored in ASCII files with one observation per line. Spaces separate fields. The dataset has been used in the American Statistical Association Statistical Graphics and Computing Sections 1999 Data Exposition.

Features:
The dataset includes numerous features covering various aspects of internet usage and user demographics. These include actual time spent online, age, community building activities, community memberships, country of residence, disabilities, education attainment, information falsification, gender, household income, how users heard about the survey, geographical location, occupation, marital status, opinions on internet issues and censorship, computing platforms, primary language, place of web access, race, reasons for not purchasing online, voter registration, sexual preference, web ordering habits, web page creation, internet access payment, willingness to pay fees, and years of internet experience.

Data Format:
The data is stored in ASCII files with one observation per line, and spaces separating fields.

Missing Values:
The dataset does not contain missing values.

This internet usage data provides valuable insights into the demographics and behaviors of early internet users, offering a snapshot of online activity and attitudes from the late 1990s. The comprehensive nature of the survey allows for in depth analysis of various factors influencing internet adoption and usage patterns during this formative period of the World Wide Web.
