America's Top College Rankings 2019 (Forbes)

Context: 
Starting in 2008, Forbes Magazine publishes a list of America's best colleges every year. Schools are ranked based on a combination of factors, including alumni salary, student satisfaction, debt, the number of American leaders among alumni, on-time graduation rate, and academic success. To learn more about the methodology, visit the Forbes website.

Content: 
The dataset contains the rankings of 650 United States colleges, along with various statistics pertaining to each school, providing a comprehensive overview of the institutions.

Acknowledgements: 
Data collected from Forbes.

Inspiration: 
Choosing where to attend college is one of the most pressing issues facing teenagers across the country. With this data, it is hoped that interesting findings will emerge regarding which schools are worth attending most, and what factors contribute to a college's success.

Features: 
Rank, College Name, City, State, Public or Private, Undergraduate Population, Student Population, Net Price, Average Grant Aid, Total Annual Cost, Alumni Salary, Acceptance Rate, SAT Scores, ACT Scores, Website

Keywords: 
College Rankings, Forbes, Higher Education, University Statistics, Academic Success
