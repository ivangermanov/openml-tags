**Data Description**

This study utilizes data from the 1988 March Current Population Survey to sample the data. The March Current Population Survey contains information on previous year's wages, schooling, industry, and occupation. We select a sample of men between the ages of eighteen and seventy with positive annual income greater than fifty dollars in nineteen ninety-two, who are not self-employed nor working without pay. The wage data is deflated by the deflator of Personal Consumption Expenditure for nineteen ninety-two. The data contains a large number of observations and has variables characterizing the individuals. The goal is to estimate the wage using information about working individuals.

**Attribute Description**

One. Wage - target feature
Two. Education - years of schooling
Three. Experience - years of potential work experience
Four. Ethnicity - race, categorized as either Caucasian or African American
Five. SMSA - whether living in a Standard Metropolitan Statistical Area, categorized as either no or yes
Six. Region - living region, categorized as either Northeast, Midwest, South, or West
Seven. Part-time - whether working part-time, categorized as either no or yes

Features: wage, education, experience, ethnicity, SMSA, region, part-time

Keywords: wage estimation, working individuals, demographic data, socioeconomic factors, labor market analysis
