Context 
The Dow Jones Industrial Average is one of the most closely followed stock market indexes by investors, financial professionals, and the media, providing a snapshot of the overall market's performance and direction.

Content 
This prominent index measures the daily price movements of thirty large American companies listed on the Nasdaq and the New York Stock Exchange, offering a broad representation of the market's trends and fluctuations. The Dow Jones Industrial Average is widely regarded as a proxy for general market conditions and even the economy of the United States, making it a closely watched indicator of the country's economic health.

This Dow Jones Industrial Average dataset is sourced from Investing.com, featuring a comprehensive collection of historical data, including closing records from the start of the year two thousand and nine to the end of the year two thousand and nineteen.

Features: 
Date, Price, Open, High, Low, Volume, Change Percentage
