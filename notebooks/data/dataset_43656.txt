Full Data Dictionary and Documentation 

Social vulnerability refers to the resilience of communities when confronted by external stresses on human health, such as natural or human caused disasters, or disease outbreaks. Reducing social vulnerability can decrease both human suffering and economic loss. 

CDC's Social Vulnerability Index uses fifteen United States census variables at the tract level to help local officials identify communities that may need support in preparing for hazards or recovering from disaster. 

The Geospatial Research, Analysis, and Services Program created and maintains CDC's Social Vulnerability Index.
