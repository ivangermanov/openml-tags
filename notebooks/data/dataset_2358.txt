Online News Popularity Dataset

Tags: Computer Systems, Machine Learning

This dataset summarizes a heterogeneous set of features about articles published by Mashable over a period of two years. The goal is to predict the number of shares in social networks, also known as popularity.

The articles were published by Mashable and their content, as well as the rights to reproduce it, belong to them. Hence, this dataset does not share the original content but provides some statistics associated with it. The original content can be publicly accessed and retrieved using the provided URLs.

Attribute Information:

The dataset contains sixty-one attributes, including fifty-eight predictive attributes, two non-predictive attributes, and one goal field.

Attribute Information includes:

* URL of the article (non-predictive)
* Days between the article publication and the dataset acquisition (non-predictive)
* Number of words in the title
* Number of words in the content
* Rate of unique words in the content
* Rate of non-stop words in the content
* Rate of unique non-stop words in the content
* Number of links
* Number of links to other articles published by Mashable
* Number of images
* Number of videos
* Average length of the words in the content
* Number of keywords in the metadata
* Data channel is Lifestyle?
* Data channel is Entertainment?
* Data channel is Business?
* Data channel is Social Media?
* Data channel is Tech?
* Data channel is World?

Other features include various keyword metrics, self-reference metrics, weekday metrics, Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) topics, global subjectivity, global sentiment polarity, rate of positive and negative words, and title subjectivity and sentiment polarity.

The target variable is the number of shares.

Relevant papers provide more information on the dataset and its applications.
