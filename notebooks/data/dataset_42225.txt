The Diamonds dataset contains the prices and various attributes of nearly fifty-four thousand diamonds. It is an excellent resource for beginners learning to work with data analysis and visualization.

Content:

* Price: The price of each diamond in United States dollars.
* Carat Weight: The weight of each diamond.
* Cut Quality: A measure of the quality of the cut, ranging from Fair to Ideal.
* Color: A rating of the diamond's color, ranging from J (the worst) to D (the best).
* Clarity: A rating of the clarity of each diamond, ranging from I-One (the worst) to Internally Flawless (the best).
* Length: The length of each diamond in millimeters.
* Width: The width of each diamond in millimeters.
* Depth: The depth of each diamond in millimeters.
* Total Depth Percentage: A calculated value representing the total depth percentage of each diamond.
* Table Width: A measure of the width of the top of each diamond relative to its widest point.

Features:

* Carat
