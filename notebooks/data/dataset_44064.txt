Online News Popularity

Tags: Computer Systems, Machine Learning

This dataset belongs to the "regression on categorical and numerical features" benchmark.

Original Description:

This version has URL set as row ID, and creator data is missing due to bad formatting.

Source: UCI

Please Cite: Proceedings of the Seventeenth EPIA 2015 - Portuguese Conference on Artificial Intelligence, September, Coimbra, Portugal.

This dataset summarizes a heterogeneous set of features about articles published by Mashable over a period of two years. The goal is to predict the number of shares in social networks, also known as popularity. The articles were published by Mashable and their content, as well as the rights to reproduce it, belongs to them. Hence, this dataset does not share the original content but some statistics associated with it. The original content can be publicly accessed and retrieved using the provided URLs.

Acquisition Date: January Eighth, Two Thousand Fifteen

The estimated relative performance values were estimated using a Random Forest classifier and a rolling window as an assessment method. See the article for more details on how the relative performance values were set.

Attribute Information:

There are sixty-one attributes in total, consisting of fifty-eight predictive attributes, two non-predictive attributes, and one goal field.

Attribute Information:

URL of the article is a non-predictive attribute.
Days between the article publication and the dataset acquisition is also a non-predictive attribute.
Number of words in the title is an important feature.
Number of words in the content is another significant feature.
Rate of unique words in the content is also crucial.
Rate of non-stop words in the content is essential.
Rate of unique non-stop words in the content is vital.
Number of links is an important aspect.
Number of links to other articles published by Mashable is also significant.
Number of images is a notable feature.
Number of videos is another notable feature.
Average length of the words in the content is a key characteristic.
Number of keywords in the metadata is vital.
Data Channel is Lifestyle, Data Channel is Entertainment, Data Channel is Business, Data Channel is Social Media, Data Channel is Tech, and Data Channel is World are all categorical features.
Worst Keyword Minimum Shares, Worst Keyword Maximum Shares, Worst Keyword Average Shares, Best Keyword Minimum Shares, Best Keyword Maximum Shares, Best Keyword Average Shares, Average Keyword Minimum Shares, Average Keyword Maximum Shares, and Average Keyword Average Shares are all related to keywords.
Minimum Shares of Referenced Articles in Mashable, Maximum Shares of Referenced Articles in Mashable, and Average Shares of Referenced Articles in Mashable are all related to referenced articles.
Was the Article Published on a Monday, Was the Article Published on a Tuesday, Was the Article Published on a Wednesday, Was the Article Published on a Thursday, Was the Article Published on a Friday, Was the Article Published on a Saturday, Was the Article Published on a Sunday, and Was the Article Published on the Weekend are all time-related features.
Closeness to Latent Dirichlet Allocation Topic Zero, Closeness to Latent Dirichlet Allocation Topic One, Closeness to Latent Dirichlet Allocation Topic Two, Closeness to Latent Dirichlet Allocation Topic Three, and Closeness to Latent Dirichlet Allocation Topic Four are all related to topic modeling.
Global Subjectivity, Global Sentiment Polarity, Global Rate of Positive Words, Global Rate of Negative Words, Rate of Positive Words Among Non-Neutral Tokens, Rate of Negative Words Among Non-Neutral Tokens, Average Polarity of Positive Words, Minimum Polarity of Positive Words, Maximum Polarity of Positive Words, Average Polarity of Negative Words, Minimum Polarity of Negative Words, Maximum Polarity of Negative Words, Title Subjectivity, Title Sentiment Polarity, Absolute Subjectivity Level, and Absolute Polarity Level are all related to sentiment analysis.
Shares is the target variable, representing the number of shares in social networks.

Keywords: online news, popularity prediction, regression, categorical and numerical features, social networks, mashable, artificial intelligence.
