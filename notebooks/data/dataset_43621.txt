The SP Bombay Stock Exchange Sensitive Index, also referred to as the BSE Thirty or simply the Sensex, is a free float market weighted stock market index of thirty well established and financially sound companies listed on the Bombay Stock Exchange. These thirty component companies, which are among the largest and most actively traded stocks, represent various industrial sectors of the Indian economy.

This dataset, known as the BSE Thirty Daily Market Price, contains data about these thirty stocks over a period of ten years, starting from May sixth, two thousand eight to May fourth, two thousand eighteen. The variables included in this dataset are the company's ticker symbol, date, opening price, highest price reached during the trading day, lowest price reached during the trading day, closing price, adjusted closing price, and the total volume of shares traded.

Acknowledgement is given to Yahoo Finance for providing the prices used in this dataset.

Some potential areas of exploration using this dataset include identifying which stocks were the most volatile or stable, as well as attempting to predict future stock prices based on historical trends.

Keywords: Bombay Stock Exchange, Sensex, stock market index, Indian economy, stock prices, volatility, prediction.
