Polish Bankruptcy One Year

The dataset is about bankruptcy prediction of Polish companies. The data was collected from Emerging Markets Information Service, which is a database containing information on emerging markets around the world. The bankrupt companies were analyzed in the period from two thousand to two thousand and twelve, while the still operating companies were evaluated from two thousand and seven to two thousand and thirteen. Based on the collected data, five classification cases were distinguished, which depend on the forecasting period.

The first year case contains financial rates from the first year of the forecasting period and corresponding class label that indicates bankruptcy status after five years. The data contains seven thousand and twenty-seven instances, two hundred and seventy-one of which represent bankrupted companies, and six thousand seven hundred and fifty-six firms that did not bankrupt in the forecasting period.

Attribute Information:

The attributes include net profit to total assets, total liabilities to total assets, working capital to total assets, current assets to short-term liabilities, and many others.

The dataset has missing values.

Additional Variable Information:

The variables include net profit to total assets, total liabilities to total assets, working capital to total assets, current assets to short-term liabilities, and many others.

Keywords: bankruptcy prediction, Polish companies, financial rates, forecasting period, classification cases.
