This dataset contains QSAR data showing activity values of several compounds on drug target Matrix Metalloproteinase Twelve. It has multiple rows and numerous features, including IDs and class features. The features represent molecular fingerprints generated from SMILES strings using the Pipeline Pilot program.

The data was obtained from the MetaQSAR project in September 2015. It is recommended to cite this dataset when used.

Matrix Metalloproteinase Twelve, also known as Macrophage Elastase, Macrophage Metalloelastase, or Matrix Metalloproteinase-Twelve, is an enzyme that belongs to the metallo protease family. It is primarily found in humans and plays a crucial role in various biological processes, including protein degradation, tissue remodeling, and immune responses.

The protein has multiple synonyms, including Macrophage Elastase, Macrophage Metalloelastase, and Matrix Metalloproteinase-Twelve. It is classified under the protein target classification of one enzyme/protease/metallo protease/metallo protease MAM clan/metallo protease M Ten A subfamily.

Several small molecules have been identified as inhibitors of Matrix Metalloproteinase Twelve, including AZD-One Two Three Six, CTS-One Zero Two Seven, and Marimastat. These compounds have been shown to inhibit the enzymatic activity of Matrix Metalloproteinase Twelve, thereby modulating its biological functions.
