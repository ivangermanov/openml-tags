The Chronic Kidney Disease Data Set is a collection of data that can be used to predict chronic kidney disease, gathered over a two-month period from a hospital.

Attribute Information:

This dataset consists of twenty-five attributes, including eleven numerical and fourteen nominal attributes. 

Age refers to the patient's age in years. 
Blood Pressure is measured in millimeters of mercury (mmHg).
Specific Gravity is categorized into five levels: 1.005, 1.010, 1.015, 1.020, and 1.025.
Albumin and Sugar are categorized into six levels: zero to five.
Red Blood Cells, Pus Cell, Pus Cell Clumps, and Bacteria are classified as either normal or abnormal, present or not present.
Blood Glucose Random is measured in milligrams per deciliter (mg/dL).
Blood Urea and Serum Creatinine are also measured in mg/dL.
Sodium and Potassium are measured in milliequivalents per liter (mEq/L).
Hemoglobin is measured in grams.
Packed Cell Volume is a percentage value.
White Blood Cell Count is measured in cells per cubic millimeter (cells/cu.mm).
Red Blood Cell Count is measured in millions per cubic millimeter (millions/cu.mm).
Hypertension, Diabetes Mellitus, Coronary Artery Disease, Appetite, Pedal Edema, and Anemia are classified as either yes or no.
Class is categorized as either chronic kidney disease (CKD) or not CKD.

Additional Information:

The dataset contains missing values.
