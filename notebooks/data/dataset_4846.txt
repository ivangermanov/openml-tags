This dataset is a subset of the National Indonesia Contraceptive Prevalence Survey. The samples consist of married women who were either not pregnant or uncertain about their pregnancy status at the time of the interview. The primary objective is to predict the current contraceptive method choice of a woman, which can be categorized into three groups - no use, long-term methods, or short-term methods - based on her demographic and socioeconomic characteristics.

Features include:

* Wife's Age
* Number of Children Ever Born
* Wife's Education
* Husband's Education
* Wife's Religion
* Whether the Wife is Currently Working
* Husband's Occupation
* Standard of Living Index
* Media Exposure
* Class

Keywords: Contraceptive Prevalence Survey, Demographic Characteristics, Socioeconomic Factors, Contraception Methods, Pregnancy Status, Married Women, Indonesia.
