FOREX EUR CHF Hourly Close Dataset 

Tags: Finance, Forex, Forex Close, Forex Hour, Life Science, Machine Learning 

This dataset contains the historical price data of the Forex EUR/CHF pair from Dukascopy. Each instance represents one hourly candlestick. The entire dataset spans from January first to December thirteenth and excludes weekends since Forex markets are not active on those days. The timestamp feature is based on the Europe/Amsterdam time zone.

The dataset description explains that each row corresponds to one hourly candlestick of the Forex EUR/CHF pair. Since Forex markets do not operate on weekends, there are no entries for those days. The time zone used for the timestamp feature is Europe/Amsterdam.

The class attribute is determined by the direction of the mean close price of both the bid and ask prices in the subsequent hour compared to the current hour. In other words, if the mean close price increases in the following hour, the class attribute is true; otherwise, it is false.

Attributes:

* Timestamp: The time of the current data point based on the Europe/Amsterdam time zone.
* Bid Open: The bid price at the beginning of the given time interval.
* Bid High: The highest bid price recorded during the specified time interval.
* Bid Low: The lowest bid price observed during the given time interval.
* Bid Close: The bid price at the end of the specified time interval.
* Bid Volume: The frequency of changes in the bid price within the given time interval.
* Ask Open: The ask price at the beginning of the given time interval.
* Ask High: The highest ask price recorded during the specified time interval.
* Ask Low: The lowest ask price observed during the given time interval.
* Ask Close: The ask price at the end of the specified time interval.
* Ask Volume: The frequency of changes in the ask price within the given time interval.
* Class: Whether the average close price will increase in the subsequent hour.
