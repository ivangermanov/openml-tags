**ADA Agnostic**

Tags: Derived, Meteorology, Transportation

The ADA Agnostic dataset is part of the Agnostic Learning vs. Prior Knowledge Challenge, which aimed to compare the performance of domain-specific feature engineering with algorithms trained on data without any domain-specific knowledge.

The dataset contains a two-class classification problem, where the goal is to identify high-revenue individuals based on census data. The original data comes from the United States Census Bureau, covering the period from June 2005 to September 2006.

In the prior knowledge track, participants had access to the original features and their identities. However, in the agnostic track, the data was preprocessed into a numeric representation, eliminating categorical variables and concealing the identity of the features.

The original dataset, known as the Adult database in the UCI Machine Learning Repository, comprises fourteen attributes, including age, work class, education, marital status, occupation, and native country. These features are a mix of continuous, binary, and categorical variables.

To create the agnostic dataset, the organizers followed specific preprocessing steps, which are detailed in the challenge documentation. The resulting dataset has forty-eight features, but these are not human-readable due to the scrambling process. The dataset includes samples from both the training and validation sets.
