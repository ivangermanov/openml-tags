**FOREX USDDKK Minute High**

Tags: Finance, Forex, Forex High, Forex Minute, Meteorology, Social Media

This dataset contains the historical price data of the US Dollar versus Danish Krone (USD/DKK) currency pair from Dukascopy.

Each instance in this dataset represents one candlestick of one minute duration. The entire dataset spans from January first to December thirteenth and excludes weekend data points since the Forex market is not active during weekends. The timestamp feature is set to the Europe/Amsterdam timezone.

The class attribute in this dataset indicates the direction of the mean high price movement between consecutive minutes. Specifically, the class attribute is true if the mean high price increases in the subsequent minute compared to the current minute, and false if the mean high price decreases or remains unchanged.

Please note that this dataset is intended for scientific purposes only and should not be used for actual trading decisions. Realistic trade strategies should be based on predictions made on close attributes, which are also available in this dataset.

The dataset consists of the following attributes:

* Timestamp: The time of the current data point in the Europe/Amsterdam timezone.
* Bid Open: The bid price at the beginning of the time interval.
* Bid High: The highest bid price recorded during the time interval.
* Bid Low: The lowest bid price recorded during the time interval.
* Bid Close: The bid price at the end of the time interval.
* Bid Volume: The number of times the bid price changed within the time interval.
* Ask Open: The ask price at the beginning of the time interval.
* Ask High: The highest ask price recorded during the time interval.
* Ask Low: The lowest ask price recorded during the time interval.
* Ask Close: The ask price at the end of the time interval.
* Ask Volume: The number of times the ask price changed within the time interval.
* Class: Whether the average high price will increase during the next time interval.
