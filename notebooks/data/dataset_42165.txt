House Prices

Ask a home buyer to describe their dream house, and they probably won't begin with the height of the basement ceiling or the proximity to an east-west railroad. But this playground competition's dataset proves that much more influences price negotiations than the number of bedrooms or a white-picket fence. With seventy-nine explanatory variables describing almost every aspect of residential homes in Ames, Iowa, this competition challenges you to predict the final price of each home.

The dataset includes features such as the type of dwelling involved in the sale, general zoning classification, lot frontage, lot area, street type, alley type, lot shape, land contour, utilities available, lot configuration, land slope, neighborhood, proximity to various conditions, type of dwelling, house style, overall material and finish of the house, overall condition of the house, original construction date, remodel date, roof style, roof material, exterior covering on house, exterior quality, exterior condition, foundation type, basement quality, basement condition, basement exposure, basement finished area, total basement square feet, heating type, heating quality, central air conditioning, electrical system, first floor square feet, second floor square feet, low quality finished square feet, above grade living area square feet, basement full bathrooms, basement half bathrooms, full bathrooms above grade, half baths above grade, bedrooms above grade, kitchens above grade, kitchen quality, total rooms above grade, home functionality, fireplaces, fireplace quality, garage type, garage year built, garage finish, garage size, garage quality, garage condition, paved driveway, wood deck area, open porch area, enclosed porch area, three season porch area, screen porch area, pool area, pool quality, fence quality, miscellaneous feature, and sale type.

The dataset also includes the month sold, year sold, and sale condition, which can be normal, abnormal, adjoining land purchase, allocation, family sale, or partial.
