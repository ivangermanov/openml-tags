This dataset, named Four Hundred Thousand New York Stock Exchange Random Investments Financial Ratios, has been tagged with context.

The dataset was created for the project "Artificial Intelligence Learns to Invest" which can be found on GitHub. The project aimed to explore the reasons behind young people's reluctance to invest in stock exchange markets.

The dataset contains over four hundred thousand randomly generated investments based on data from the New York Stock Exchange market over the past decade. Financial ratios and volatilities were calculated and added to the random investments dataset. These financial ratios include Environmental, Social, and Governance Ranking, Return on Assets, Return on Equity, Net Yearly Income, Price to Book, Price to Earnings, Price to Sales, Earnings Per Share, and Sharpe Ratio.

The dataset includes features such as Company, Sector, Horizon in Days, Amount, Date of Purchase, Date of Sale, Purchase Price, Sale Price, Volatility at Purchase, Volatility at Sale, Sharpe Ratio, Expected Annual Return, Inflation Rate, Nominal Return, Investment Value, Environmental, Social, and Governance Ranking, Price to Earnings Ratio, Earnings Per Share Ratio, Price to Sales Ratio, Price to Book Ratio, Net Profit Margin Ratio, Current Ratio, Return on Assets Ratio, and Return on Equity Ratio.

Keywords: Artificial Intelligence, Investments, Financial Ratios, New York Stock Exchange, Stock Market, Young Investors, Data Science.
