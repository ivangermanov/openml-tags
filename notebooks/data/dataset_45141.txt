Child Bayesian Network. Sample.

The bnlearn Bayesian Network Repository reference is a valuable resource for this dataset.

The Child Bayesian Network is a comprehensive model consisting of twenty nodes and twenty-five arcs, with a total of parameters. The average Markov blanket size is approximately three, and the average degree is two point five. The maximum in-degree is two.

This dataset is a classic example of a probabilistic expert system, showcasing the power of Bayesian networks in modeling complex relationships.

The Child Bayesian Network features a range of variables, including Age, Disease, and Lung Function, among others.
