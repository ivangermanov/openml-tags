Heart Disease Prediction

Context: Heart disease is the leading cause of death in the developed world, making it a critical health issue that requires urgent attention. As a result, it is essential to identify the risks associated with heart disease and take preventive measures to mitigate them.

Content: This dataset aims to predict which patients are most likely to suffer from heart disease in the near future based on various features. By analyzing these features, we can identify high-risk patients and provide them with timely interventions to prevent heart attacks or strokes.

Acknowledgement: This data comes from the University of California Irvine's Machine Learning Repository.

Features: Age, Sex, Chest Pain Type, Blood Pressure, Cholesterol Level, Fasting Blood Sugar, Electrocardiogram Results, Maximum Heart Rate, Exercise Angina, ST Depression, Slope of ST, Number of Vessels, Thallium Scan, and Heart Disease Status.

Keywords: Heart Disease, Prediction, Risk Factors, Prevention, Machine Learning, Health Analytics.
