Title of Database: Abalone Data

Sources:
Original Owners of Database: Marine Resources Division Marine Research Laboratories - Taroona Department of Primary Industry and Fisheries, Tasmania 
Donor of Database: Department of Computer Science, University of Tasmania 
Date Received: December 

Past Usage: Extensive research has been conducted on this dataset, including a Ph.D. thesis by Sam Waugh in 1995, titled "Extending and Benchmarking Cascade-Correlation". The thesis reported test set performances of various machine learning algorithms, including Cascade-Correlation, C4.5, Linear Discriminant Analysis, and k-Nearest Neighbors.

Relevant Information: This dataset aims to predict the age of abalone from physical measurements. The age of abalone is typically determined by cutting the shell, staining it, and counting the number of rings under a microscope. However, this process is tedious and time-consuming. Therefore, researchers have resorted to using other easily measurable characteristics to predict the age. It is worth noting that further information, such as weather patterns and location, might be necessary to accurately solve this problem. 

Data Characteristics: The dataset contains four thousand one hundred seventy-seven instances, each described by eight attributes. Seven of these attributes are continuous, while one is nominal. There are no missing values in the dataset. 

Attribute Information:

* Sex: Nominal attribute indicating the sex of the abalone.
* Length: Continuous attribute representing the longest shell measurement in millimeters.
* Diameter: Continuous attribute representing the diameter of the abalone in millimeters, measured perpendicular to the length.
* Height: Continuous attribute representing the height of the abalone in millimeters, measured with the meat still inside the shell.
* Whole Weight: Continuous attribute representing the weight of the entire abalone in grams.
* Shucked Weight: Continuous attribute representing the weight of the abalone meat in grams.
* Viscera Weight: Continuous attribute representing the weight of the abalone's gut in grams, measured after bleeding.
* Shell Weight: Continuous attribute representing the weight of the abalone's shell in grams, measured after drying.
* Rings: Integer attribute indicating the number of rings, which corresponds to the age of the abalone plus 1.5 years.

Statistics for Numeric Domains:
The statistics for the numeric domains include minimum, maximum, mean, standard deviation, and correlation coefficients.

Class Distribution:
The class distribution shows that the data is highly imbalanced, with most instances belonging to classes 8, 9, and 10.

Keywords: Abalone, Age Prediction, Physical Measurements, Machine Learning, Classification Problem, Regression Problem, Imbalanced Dataset.
