This dataset contains QSAR data showing activity values of several compounds on drug target Thromboxane A2 Receptor, and it has multiple rows and features, including IDs and class feature. The features represent FCFP 1024-bit Molecular Fingerprints which were generated from SMILES strings.

The dataset is part of the MetaQSAR project, specifically Study Three, and was released in September 2015.

Thromboxane A2 Receptor is a single protein, also known as Prostanoid TP Receptor or TBXA2R, and belongs to the species group Homo sapiens. It is classified as a membrane receptor, specifically a Family A G protein-coupled receptor, and is involved in the signaling pathway of lipid-like ligands.

The receptor plays a crucial role in various physiological processes, including smooth muscle contraction, inflammatory response, signal transduction, and response to nutrients. It is also implicated in certain diseases, such as bleeding disorders and cardiovascular disease.

Several compounds have been identified as antagonists or inhibitors of Thromboxane A2 Receptor, including Ramatroban, Picotamide, Terutroban, Seratrodast, and Terbogrel. These compounds have undergone various phases of clinical trials and have shown potential therapeutic effects.
