Tags: Drug Design, Molecular Descriptors, Structure-Activity Relationships

This dataset is one of drug design datasets formed using Adriana.Code software. The datasets contain features derived from molecular structures and properties. The molecules and outputs are taken from original studies on various drug compounds and chemical properties.

The dataset focuses on quantitative structure-activity relationships (QSAR) and structure-property relationships for different classes of molecules. It includes data on melting points, biological activity, toxicity, and biodegradability of various chemical compounds. The features were generated to capture molecular properties relevant for drug design and development.

Some of the original studies examined properties of carbolenes, benzodiazepine receptor ligands, polyaromatic hydrocarbons, protein kinase inhibitors, anilines, phenols, halogenated aliphatic compounds, and other drug-like molecules. The datasets cover a wide range of chemical space relevant to medicinal chemistry and toxicology.

The features in the dataset represent various molecular descriptors calculated from the chemical structures. These likely include topological, geometrical, electronic, and physicochemical properties of the molecules that are useful for building predictive QSAR models.

This particular dataset appears to focus on a specific class of compounds, possibly related to previous studies on similar molecular scaffolds. It can be used to develop and validate computational models for predicting properties and activities of drug-like molecules based on their structural features.
