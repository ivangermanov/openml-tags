Demographic and Socio-Economic Context UNESCO

This dataset provides a complete set of demographic and socioeconomic variables.

Sources:
United Nations, Department of Economic and Social Affairs, Population Division
Eurostat (Demographic Statistics)
National Statistical Offices
The World Bank, World Development Indicators
International Monetary Fund, World Economic Outlook database
United Nations Statistics Division, National Accounts Main Aggregates Database

Content Variables Defined:

Demographic Variables:

* Total Fertility Rate (births per woman)
* Life Expectancy at Birth (years)
* Infant Mortality Rate (per one thousand live births)
* Population Aged Fourteen Years or Younger
* Population Aged Fifteen to Twenty-Four Years
* Population Aged Twenty-Five to Sixty-Four Years
* Population Aged Sixty-Five Years or Older
* Annual Population Growth
* Prevalence of HIV (percentage of population aged fifteen to forty-nine)
* Rural Population (percentage of total population)
* Total Population

Socio-Economic Variables:

* Total Debt Service (percentage of Gross National Income)
* GDP Deflator (base year varies by country)
* Annual GDP Growth
* GDP in Purchasing Power Parity (current international dollars)
* GDP per Capita (current US dollars)
* GDP per Capita in Purchasing Power Parity (current international dollars)
* GNI per Capita, Atlas Method (current US dollars)
* GNI per Capita in Purchasing Power Parity (current international dollars)
* PPP Conversion Factor, GDP (local currency units per international dollar)
* Poverty Headcount Ratio at One Point Ninety a Day (percentage of population)
* General Government Total Expenditure (current local currency units)

Keywords: demographic variables, socioeconomic variables, population growth, GDP, poverty rate, HIV prevalence, rural population, total debt service.
