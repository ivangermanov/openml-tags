**FOREX Eur Jpy Minute High**

Tags: Chemistry, Finance, Forex, Forex High, Forex Minute, Machine Learning

**Source:** Dukascopy Historical Data Feed

**Data Description**
This dataset contains the historical price data of the Forex Eur Jpy currency pair from Dukascopy. Each instance represents one candlestick of one minute duration. The entire dataset spans a period from January first to December thirteenth and excludes weekends, as the Forex market is not traded during these days. The timestamp feature is set to the Europe Amsterdam time zone.

The class attribute signifies the direction of the mean high price movement between the current minute and the subsequent minute. Specifically, the class attribute is true when the mean high price increases in the following minute, and false when it decreases or remains stagnant.

**Note that this is a hypothetical task, intended solely for scientific purposes. Realistic trading strategies should only be applied to predictions based on close attributes, which are also available.**

**Attributes**
* Timestamp: The time of the current data point, set to the Europe Amsterdam time zone.
* Bid Open: The bid price at the beginning of this time interval.
* Bid High: The highest bid price recorded during this time interval.
* Bid Low: The lowest bid price observed during this time interval.
* Bid Close: The bid price at the conclusion of this time interval.
* Bid Volume: The frequency of changes to the bid price within this time interval.
* Ask Open: The ask price at the commencement of this time interval.
* Ask High: The highest ask price attained during this time interval.
* Ask Low: The lowest ask price recorded during this time interval.
* Ask Close: The ask price at the termination of this time interval.
* Ask Volume: The frequency of changes to the ask price within this time interval.
* Class: Indicates whether the average price will increase during the subsequent interval.
