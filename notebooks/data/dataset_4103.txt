Online News Popularity Dataset

Tags: Computer Systems, Mathematics

This dataset belongs to the "regression on categorical and numerical features" benchmark.

The Online News Popularity dataset summarizes a heterogeneous set of features about articles published by Mashable over a period of two years. The goal is to predict the number of shares in social networks, which is referred to as popularity.

The articles were published by Mashable and their content, including the rights to reproduce it, belongs to them. Hence, this dataset does not share the original content but provides some statistics associated with it. The original content can be publicly accessed and retrieved using the provided URLs.

Acquisition Date: January 8, 2015

Attribute Information:

* URL of the Article: a non-predictive attribute
* Time Delta: days between the article publication and the dataset acquisition, also a non-predictive attribute
* Number of Words in the Title
* Number of Words in the Content
* Rate of Unique Words in the Content
* Rate of Non-Stop Words in the Content
* Rate of Unique Non-Stop Words in the Content
* Number of Links
* Number of Links to Other Articles Published by Mashable
* Number of Images
* Number of Videos
* Average Length of the Words in the Content
* Number of Keywords in the Metadata
* Data Channel Categories:
	+ Lifestyle
	+ Entertainment
	+ Business
	+ Social Media
	+ Tech
	+ World
* Keyword Statistics:
	+ Minimum Shares of the Worst Keyword
	+ Maximum Shares of the Worst Keyword
	+ Average Shares of the Worst Keyword
	+ Minimum Shares of the Best Keyword
	+ Maximum Shares of the Best Keyword
	+ Average Shares of the Best Keyword
	+ Average Shares of the Average Keyword
* Self Reference Statistics:
	+ Minimum Shares of Referenced Articles in Mashable
	+ Maximum Shares of Referenced Articles in Mashable
	+ Average Shares of Referenced Articles in Mashable
* Day of the Week the Article was Published:
	+ Monday
	+ Tuesday
	+ Wednesday
	+ Thursday
	+ Friday
	+ Saturday
	+ Sunday
	+ Weekend
* Latent Dirichlet Allocation Topic Modeling:
	+ Closeness to Topic 0
	+ Closeness to Topic 1
	+ Closeness to Topic 2
	+ Closeness to Topic 3
	+ Closeness to Topic 4
* Text Sentiment Analysis:
	+ Global Subjectivity
	+ Global Sentiment Polarity
	+ Rate of Positive Words in the Content
	+ Rate of Negative Words in the Content
	+ Average Polarity of Positive Words
	+ Minimum Polarity of Positive Words
	+ Maximum Polarity of Positive Words
	+ Average Polarity of Negative Words
	+ Minimum Polarity of Negative Words
	+ Maximum Polarity of Negative Words
* Title Sentiment Analysis:
	+ Title Subjectivity
	+ Title Sentiment Polarity
	+ Absolute Subjectivity Level
	+ Absolute Polarity Level
* Target Variable:
	+ Number of Shares

Keywords: online news, popularity prediction, social network shares, article features, sentiment analysis, topic modeling.
