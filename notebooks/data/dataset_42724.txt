Online News Popularity Tags: Version with URL set as row ID, creator data missing due to bad formatting.

This dataset summarizes a heterogeneous set of features about articles published by Mashable over a period of two years. The goal is to predict the number of shares in social networks, or popularity. The articles were published by Mashable and their content, as the rights to reproduce it, belongs to them. Hence, this dataset does not share the original content but some statistics associated with it. The original content can be publicly accessed and retrieved using the provided URLs.

Acquisition date was January 8, 2015. The estimated relative performance values were estimated using a Random Forest classifier and a rolling windows as assessment method. See the article for more details on how the relative performance values were set.

Attribute Information: The dataset has sixty-one attributes, including fifty-eight predictive attributes, two non-predictive attributes, and one goal field.

The non-predictive attributes are the URL of the article and the days between the article publication and the dataset acquisition.

The predictive attributes include the number of words in the title, the number of words in the content, the rate of unique words in the content, the rate of non-stop words in the content, the rate of unique non-stop words in the content, the number of links, the number of links to other articles published by Mashable, the number of images, the number of videos, the average length of the words in the content, the number of keywords in the metadata, and whether the data channel is Lifestyle, Entertainment, Business, Social Media, Tech, or World.

Additionally, the dataset includes attributes such as the worst keyword, best keyword, and average keyword, as well as the minimum, maximum, and average shares of referenced articles in Mashable. It also includes attributes related to the day of the week the article was published, such as whether it was a Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday, or Sunday, and whether it was a weekend.

The dataset also includes attributes related to the text subjectivity, sentiment polarity, rate of positive and negative words, and the average, minimum, and maximum polarity of positive and negative words. It also includes attributes related to the title subjectivity, sentiment polarity, and absolute subjectivity and polarity levels.

The goal field is the number of shares, which is the target variable to be predicted.
