**PHENETYL1**

This dataset is part of a collection of forty-one drug design datasets, each featuring one hundred fourteen three attributes generated using Adriana.Code software. The molecules and outputs were sourced from the original studies listed below.

The original studies include Carbolenes, MTP2, Chang, Cristalli, Depreux, Doherty, Garrat2, Garrat, Heyl, Krystek, Lewis, Penning, Rosowsky, Siddiqi, Stevenson, Strupcz, Svensson, Thompson, Tsutumi, Uejling, Yokoyama1, and Yokoyama2.

Carbolenes originated from "Journal of Medicinal Chemistry" in nineteen ninety-six. MTP2 came from "Journal of Chemical Information and Computer Science" in two thousand three.

Chang, Cristalli, Depreux, Doherty, Garrat2, Garrat, Heyl, Krystek, Lewis, Penning, Rosowsky, Siddiqi, Stevenson, Strupcz, Svensson, Thompson, Tsutumi, Uejling, Yokoyama1, and Yokoyama2 were taken from "Journal of Medicinal Chemistry" in nineteen ninety-six.

Benzo thirty-two originated from "European Journal of Medicinal Chemistry" in nineteen ninety-six. PHENETYL1 was sourced from "QSAR: Hansch Analysis and Related Approaches" published in nineteen ninety-three.

Pah was taken from "Chemometrics and Intelligent Laboratory Systems" in nineteen ninety-five. PDGFR came from "Journal of Chemical Information and Computer Science" in two thousand four.

Phen was sourced from "Journal of Medicinal Chemistry" in nineteen seventy-two. Topo Two One and Yprop Four One originated from "Journal of Chemical Information and Computer Science" in two thousand three.

Qsabr One and Qsabr Two were taken from "Chemosphere" in nineteen ninety-seven. Qsartox originated from "Chemosphere" in nineteen ninety-eight.

Qsbr Rw One was sourced from "Biodegradation of Dioxins and Furans" published in nineteen ninety-eight. Qsbr Y Two was taken from "Biodegradability Prediction" published in nineteen ninety-eight.

Qsbr Alks originated from "Quantitative Structure-Activity Relationships" in nineteen ninety-eight. Qsf Rdhla was sourced from "Quantitative Structure-Activity Relationships" in nineteen ninety-seven.

Qsfsr One and Qsfsr Two were taken from "Protein Engineering" in nineteen ninety-eight. Qspr Cmpx originated from "Journal of Chemical Information and Computer Science" in two thousand.

Selwood was sourced from "Journal of Medicinal Chemistry" in nineteen ninety.
