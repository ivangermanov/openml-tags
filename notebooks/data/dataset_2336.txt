The KDD Internet Usage dataset contains general demographic information on internet users in 1997. 

This dataset was originally owned by the Graphics, Visualization, and Usability Center, College of Computing, Georgia Institute of Technology, Atlanta, GA.

The data comes from a survey conducted by the Graphics and Visualization Unit at Georgia Tech from October 10 to November 16, 1997. 

The particular subset of the survey provided here is the general demographics of internet users. 

The data has been recoded as entirely numeric, with an index to the codes described in the coding file.

The full survey is available, along with summaries, tables, and graphs of their analyses. 

In addition, there is information on other parts of the survey, including technology demographics and web commerce.

Features include actual time, age, community building, community membership, country, disability, education attainment, falsification of information, gender, household income, how you heard about the survey, major geographical location, major occupation, marital status, most important issue facing the internet, opinions on censorship, primary computing platform, primary language, primary place of WWW access, race, reasons for not purchasing online, registered to vote, sexual preference, web ordering, web page creation, who pays for access, willingness to pay fees, years on internet.

Keywords: internet usage, demographics, survey, Georgia Tech, technology, web commerce.
