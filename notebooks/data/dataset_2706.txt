**FOREX Eur Try Day High**

Tags: Economics, Finance, Forex, Forex Day, Forex High, Life Science

**Source:** Dukascopy Historical Data Feed

**Data Description**
This dataset contains the historical price data of the Forex Eur/Try from Dukascopy. Each instance represents one candlestick of one day. The entire dataset spans a specific period of time, excluding weekends since Forex markets are closed during these days. The timestamp feature follows the Europe/Amsterdam timezone.

The class attribute signifies the direction of the mean high price compared to the previous day. Specifically, it indicates whether the mean high price will rise or fall (or remain unchanged) in the subsequent trading day.

**Note:** This dataset is intended for scientific purposes only, and realistic trading strategies should be based on predictions made on close attributes, which are also available in this dataset.

**Attributes**

* **Timestamp**: The time of the current data point, adhering to the Europe/Amsterdam timezone.
* **Bid Open**: The bid price at the commencement of this time interval.
* **Bid High**: The highest bid price recorded during this time interval.
* **Bid Low**: The lowest bid price observed during this time interval.
* **Bid Close**: The bid price at the conclusion of this time interval.
* **Bid Volume**: The frequency of changes in the bid price within this time interval.
* **Ask Open**: The ask price at the initiation of this time interval.
* **Ask High**: The highest ask price registered during this time interval.
* **Ask Low**: The lowest ask price observed during this time interval.
* **Ask Close**: The ask price at the termination of this time interval.
* **Ask Volume**: The frequency of changes in the ask price within this time interval.
* **Class**: An indicator of whether the average price will ascend or descend (or remain static) during the subsequent interval.
