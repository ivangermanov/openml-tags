QSAR-TID-72 Dataset

This dataset contains QSAR data showing activity values of several compounds on drug target TID: 72. The features represent FCFP 1024bit Molecular Fingerprints which were generated from SMILES strings.

The dataset has 5354 rows and 1026 features, including IDs and class feature: MOLECULE CHEMBL ID and MEDIAN PX50.

Generating Fingerprints does not usually require missing value imputation as all bits are generated.

The dataset is part of the MetaQSAR project.

Dopamine D2 Receptor

The Dopamine D2 receptor is a single protein that is a dopamine receptor. It is also known as D(2) dopamine receptor and is a membrane receptor that belongs to the family A G protein-coupled receptor.

The receptor is involved in various biological processes, including temperature homeostasis, response to hypoxia, neuron migration, and synaptic transmission.

It is also involved in various diseases, including myoclonic dystonia.

The receptor has been targeted by various compounds, including JNJ-37822681, Benperidol, Levosulpiride, Tiapride, and Aplindore.

These compounds have been shown to have various effects on the receptor, including antagonism and partial agonism.

The receptor has been studied in various organisms, including Homo sapiens.
